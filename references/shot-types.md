# The 7 UGC Shot Types

UGC isn't infinite. Real creator accounts run a small, predictable vocabulary of
formats, and that's a feature, because it means you can encode them once and
compose rather than authoring each shot from scratch. Pick a shot type, load its
defaults, and the 6-layer assembler fills in around the identity card.

Each type below lists its default **camera profile**, typical **scenario** seeds,
and what it's good for.

---

## 1. Testimonial
- **Camera:** selfie front cam
- **Scenario seeds:** holding/showing a product, talking to camera, reacting
- **Use for:** product reviews, "this changed my routine," ad-style endorsements
- **Note:** the most monetizable format: the actor looks into the lens and talks.

## 2. Lifestyle
- **Camera:** rear cam (sometimes front)
- **Scenario seeds:** walking, coffee, beach, getting ready, candid moment
- **Use for:** building the character's "life," top-of-funnel vibe content
- **Note:** light on selling, heavy on relatability.

## 3. Unboxing
- **Camera:** overhead flatlay or close rear cam
- **Scenario seeds:** opening a parcel, first reactions, hands in frame
- **Use for:** product reveals, PR hauls, "look what I got"

## 4. Get-Ready-With-Me (GRWM)
- **Camera:** mirror selfie
- **Scenario seeds:** mid-makeup, half-done hair, choosing an outfit
- **Use for:** routine content, slow-burn product placement during the process

## 5. Product demo
- **Camera:** rear cam, close-up
- **Scenario seeds:** applying the product, before/after, hands and face focus
- **Use for:** showing how something works (skincare, makeup, gadgets)

## 6. Haul / try-on
- **Camera:** full-length mirror selfie
- **Scenario seeds:** outfit changes, holding items up, multiple looks
- **Use for:** fashion, multiple SKUs in one piece

## 7. Day-in-the-life
- **Camera:** mixed profiles, stitched sequence
- **Scenario seeds:** morning → outfit → out-and-about → evening
- **Use for:** a mini-vlog narrative; the strongest format for making the
  synthetic person feel like a real continuous human.

---

## Composing a week

A content calendar is just a mix of these. Example seven one-line briefs:

```
Lila, morning coffee, oversized knit, window light        # lifestyle
Lila, gym mirror selfie, athleisure, post-workout flush   # lifestyle / mirror
Lila, unboxing skincare, kitchen counter, overhead        # unboxing
Lila, GRWM, bathroom mirror, half-done makeup             # GRWM
Lila, testimonial, holding serum, soft front light        # testimonial
Lila, beach walk, linen dress, golden hour                # lifestyle
Lila, night out, restaurant lighting, mirror selfie       # lifestyle / mirror
```

Same person in every frame because every frame compiles from the same card. You
don't reinvent "what does an unboxing look like" each time. You call the type.
