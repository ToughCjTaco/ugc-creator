# iPhone Camera Simulation Profiles

The second realism lever (after the anchors) is the **capture device**.
"Cinematic" is a tell. Real UGC looks like it was filmed on a phone in someone's
bathroom by a tired person with one hand: because it was. So Layer 4 of every
prompt picks one of these four profiles and inlines its character. You're not
choosing a pretty angle; you're choosing a believable capture device.

Each profile carries its own focal feel, framing logic, grain signature, and
handheld behavior. Pick the one that matches the shot type.

---

## 1. Selfie front cam

The arm's-length, talking-to-camera look. Default for testimonials and
talking-head UGC.

```
Shot on iPhone front selfie camera: arm's-length framing, subject fills the frame
from chest up, slight upward or straight-on tilt, mild wide-angle lens distortion
(nose/center slightly enlarged), softer front-sensor detail, visible handheld
micro-shake, vertical 9:16.
```

## 2. Rear cam ("someone filmed me")

Sharper and cleaner than the front cam, with main-lens focal compression. Reads as
"a friend filmed this." Good for lifestyle and product demos.

```
Shot on iPhone rear main camera: sharper detail, natural focal compression,
filmed by someone a few feet away, candid framing with subject slightly
off-center, shallow natural depth of field, vertical 9:16.
```

## 3. Mirror selfie

Phone visible in hand, reflection softness and glare, room context. The classic
get-ready / outfit-check format.

```
Mirror selfie shot on iPhone: phone visible in one hand, slight reflection
softness and glass glare, faint smudges on the mirror, bathroom or bedroom
context behind her, flash bloom optional, vertical 9:16.
```

## 4. Overhead flatlay

Top-down product shot. Even-ish lighting, hands entering frame, slight imperfect
alignment. For unboxing and product-on-surface content.

```
Overhead flatlay shot on iPhone: top-down view of a surface, product and props
arranged slightly imperfectly, hands entering the frame, even soft lighting with
mild lens distortion creeping in at the edges, vertical 9:16 or square.
```

---

## Choosing fast

| Shot intent | Profile |
|-------------|---------|
| Talking to camera, testimonial | Selfie front cam |
| Candid lifestyle, "a friend filmed me", product demo | Rear cam |
| Outfit check, GRWM, room context | Mirror selfie |
| Product on a surface, unboxing | Overhead flatlay |

When in doubt for a UGC influencer, the **front selfie cam** is the workhorse:
most real creator content is arm's-length and talking.
